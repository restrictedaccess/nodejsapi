Payment Terms And Conditions
 (These factors from the Remote Staff Service Agreement will help you understand our accounting policies)
  1. Date Coverage on your invoice: Remote Staff invoices are issued in advance for prepaid and regular monthly billings. The invoices are calculated based on your Remote Staff contractors daily wages, assuming always that there are 22 working days each month. Regular work hours are 8 hours (full-time) or 4 hours (part-time) daily. The advanced invoices issued cannot factor variables ahead of time, such as overtime or undertime. This is why the date coverage on your Remote Staff invoice is tentative and is only an indication of how many working days can be expected ahead. For those on prepaid billing: when your credit runs low, the system will automatically generate an advanced invoice for you, which may have crossover dates for the date coverage.

  2. Fixed Contract Rate and Currency Adjustments: We apply a fixed contract rate at the time of hire; we publish the fixed contract rates we apply to AUD/USD/GBP currencies in the Remote Staff Service Agreement. This fixed contract rate will always be used as a reference when invoices are issued. The fixed contract rate helps Remote Staff keep hourly rates of staff the same on all invoices. The fixed contract rate is adjustable by customer request every six months but this will change the hourly rate on your invoices; otherwise the fixed contract rate will remain fixed for the life of the working relationship. Every time a new invoice is issued we apply an independent average forex rate based on the actual live forex market rate at the time when your invoice was generated. We then adjust this against your fixed contract rate to determine how much credit or debit will be applied to every invoice issued. We apply your currency credit or debit to your available running balance. We refer to this as the currency adjustment. We have currency adjustments in place so that we can issue invoices in our customers local currencies, and pay staff in their local currencies.

  3. 3 Methods of billing and suspensions: We have three methods of billing: 1) Prepaid billing invoices, 2) Advanced monthly invoices and 3) Advanced monthly itemised invoices. All billing methods are in place because Remote Staff needs your account to always remain in credit in order for us to deliver our service to you. Our advanced billing system allows us to guarantee pay to staff, which is an integral part of our service delivery promise. Once your available running balance is negative or if your advanced monthly invoice isnt paid on time, Remote Staff has the right to suspend contracted staff from working with you until the outstanding invoice is paid or until your available running balance is back in credit.

    Prepaid billing: With prepaid billing, our fees are conveniently embedded in the hourly rate of your contractor and are all-inclusive ?Currency adjustments are shown separately ?Credit is removed from your account at the end of every working day, however your account is reconciled with any over/undertime at the end of every week ?Reconciliations at the end of every week are referred to as time sheet adjustments ?You can choose to be invoiced on a weekly, fortnightly or monthly basis or you can pay-as-you-go and manage the amounts you want to pay ?After payment, our system will automatically generate an invoice/receipt in arrears ?If at any month, 2 or more suspensions occur due to insufficient credit, Remote Staff will apply a USD$150 admin charge to your available running balance.

    Advanced monthly itemised billing: For those with office-based 3+ teams, your itemised invoice shows hourly wages, service fees, office fees and currency adjustments separately ?Your account is balanced out at the end of the month and any necessary adjustments apply in next month's invoice.

  4. Adjustment of time sheets: We make adjustments to time sheets for a variety of reasons, such as if staff forget to log out of the system or if they worked overtime without approval. We also remove any hours of unproven work. Prepaid billing, any overtime/undertime + currency adjustments are made at the end of each day, but are reconciled at the end of every week. Advanced monthly billing, you are billed in advance for the month, your time sheets are then reconciled at the end of the month and any necessary adjustments are applied in the following invoice.

  5. How we apply Remote Staff service fees: We have two ways in which we apply our service fees, embedded and itemised.

    Embedded. Our service fees are not percentage-based but are applied on a sliding scale dependent on skill level, part-time/full-time status, and level of involvement needed. Although the range can be significant, our service fees are very competitive in the outsourcing market. We only earn our service fee when our staff are working.

    Itemised. For those with office-based staff, itemised invoices disclose our service fees showing separated hourly rates of staff wages, office fees and service charges. These are fixed fees without variables, $330 per person per month.

  6. GST. As an Australian company, we apply GST as a separate item on our invoices for all Australian clients. There is no GST charge for international clients.

  7. Mandatory paid leave: In the Philippines, employees receive a government man-dated end-of-year bonus, known as the 13th month pay. As an international employer who hires contractors from Remote Staff, you are not required to provide this entitlement. However, since 2013 it has been our company policy that all clients pay a mandatory five or six day paid leave entitlement, as a token to offset this expectation. We applied such an entitlement because of increasing global competition. We want you to remain an employer of choice and providing this to your staff will help you retain the best Filipino talent. This entitlement is applied to your December invoice only. Please note that Christmas bonuses can be paid on top of the mandatory paid leave, but the five or six day mandatory paid leave has to be paid first. If you employ a staff member after Oct 1st of any year, then this is optional for that year. However, it will be mandatory for the succeeding years. Outside of the Christmas period, the 'no work no pay' rule still applies.

  8. Refunds and cancellations:

    Refunds. Your available running balance with Remote Staff is your credit to be consumed or refunded. Unless its consumed, its your money indefinitely. We have heavily invested in our technology to ensure that hours and credit consumed are genuinely worked. Any credit that is not consumed, can be fully refunded within approximately 2 weeks. Your running balance is not charged unless there are hours of proven work, or unless you have approved for your staff to work off the Remote Staff platform system or offline (not advisable). Consumed hours are not refundable but can be questioned before payroll is made. During that time, we suspend payment until the investigation is complete. This process can take up to 6 weeks.

    Cancellations. There is a 24-hour termination notice period within the first six months. For hires over six months, there is a 2-week termination period in which case you need to have credit for the equivalent of 2 weeks salary during that time. In both cases a full refund is given of any unconsumed credit remaining thereafter. At the end of every working relationship, a statement of account is made to determine how much debit/credit is yours. A final invoice is generated detailing how much will be refunded or if you need to pay any outstanding balances.

  9. Invoice amendments:

    Valid reasons. 1) When one or more staff members are ending his/her contract. A full statement of account will be generated and an amendment on your existing invoice will be applied as we remove the advanced hours of work billed for that staff member. 2) Changes to your fixed contract rate. If you change your fixed contract rate you will change your hourly rate of staff wages, we will then reissue an amended invoice. 3) Additional add-ons such as gifts for birthdays or work equipment upgrades. 4) Pay increase. If there has been an invoice issued but you have increased the staff pay, an updated invoice can be generated

    Invalid reasons. 1) For leave requests such as leave of absence, sick leave or holidays. For prepaid billing, if your contractor is sick or is not working for whatever reason, no credit is removed from your account. Your credit remains as is. For advanced monthly billing, these amendments will appear on the following invoice as credit so theres no need to amend them. 2) Commissions. Its best that commissions are invoiced separately and that no amendments to regular invoices are made. Commissions are usually paid after the fact, in arrears.

  11. For invoices in USD and other currencies, the merchant facility fees are 2.75% for all credit card payments. AMEX is not accepted.
